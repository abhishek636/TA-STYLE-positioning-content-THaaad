Usefulness of Cats

In our urban and suburban houses what should we do without cats? In our sitting or bedrooms, our libraries, in our kitchns and storerooms, our farms, barns, and rickyard, in our docks, our granaries, our ships, and other places too numerous to mention, how useful they are! In our ships, howere, the rats oft set them at defiance; still they are of great service.

How wonderfully patient is the cat when watching for rats or mics, awaiting their egress from their place of refuge or that which is their home!How well Shakespeare in paricles,Act iii., describes this keen attention of the cat to its natural pursuit

The cat, with eyes of burning coal,Now crouches from (before) the mouse's hole.

A slight rustle, and the fugitive comes forth; a quick, sharp, resolute motion, and the cat has proved its usefulness. Let any one have a plague of rats and mice, as I once had, and let them be delivered therefrom by cats, as i was, and they will have a lasting and king regard for them.
